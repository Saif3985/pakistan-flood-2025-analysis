256
Buner District — Worst Single Event
One cloudburst on July 13–28 killed 256 people — more than any other district in Pakistan. Flash flood in steep mountain valley with no escape routes.
976 km²
Jhang — Most Flooded District
Jhang (Punjab) had 976 km² under water — the largest flooded area — but only 1 death. Slow riverine flood gave people time to evacuate.
2 phases
Two-Phase Monsoon Structure
Phase 1 (Jun 26–Jul 31): KPK flash floods — 23 deaths/district avg. Phase 2 (Aug 1–Sep 30): Punjab riverine — 10.5 deaths/district avg. Separated by ~2-week gap.
25%
Children Deaths — SDG Vulnerability
Children accounted for 25% of all flood deaths — disproportionate to their population share. Relevant to SDG 13 (Climate Action) and SDG 3 (Good Health).
11,534 km²
Total SAR Flood Extent
Sentinel-1 SAR detected 11,534 km² flooded across Pakistan in full 2025 monsoon season. Phase 2 (Punjab) covered 13% more land than Phase 1 (KPK).
TYPE > EXTENT
Core Research Finding
Flood TYPE determines mortality, not flood EXTENT. Flash floods kill more per km² than riverine floods. Satellite area alone cannot predict human impact.
